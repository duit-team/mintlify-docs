**Share Commerce ePay** is the online payment gateway that lets customers pay by card, e-wallet, and other methods when ordering through your QR code / online ordering page. This guide walks through setting up that integration.

<Note>
  Setting up a physical Share Commerce card terminal instead? That's a different processor called just **Share Commerce** (no "ePay") — see [Payment Types](/features/payment-types#two-share-commerce-processors) to add it. It just needs the terminal's IP address, port, and model — no online merchant account, and this guide won't apply to it.
</Note>

## What You'll Need

- **Merchant Account**: Active Share Commerce merchant account with your business verified
- **Merchant ID**: Available in your Share Commerce merchant dashboard
- **Secret Key**: API credential used to sign and verify payment requests (treat like a password) — this same key is used for both the redirect callback and the webhook, there is no separate webhook secret to configure
- **Environment**: Whether your Merchant ID and Secret Key were issued for Staging (testing) or Production (live) — DuitPOS needs to know which so it calls the right Share Commerce endpoint

## Step 1: Get Your Credentials from Share Commerce

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Share Commerce Dashboard">
    Go to [Share Commerce Merchant Portal](https://merchant.sharecommerce.com) and log in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find Your Merchant ID">
    Navigate to **Settings → Merchant Account** and copy your **Merchant ID**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy Your Secret Key">
    Go to **Settings → API Keys** and copy your **API Secret Key** — use the staging key while testing, and the production key only when you switch the payment type to Production.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2: Configure Share Commerce ePay in DuitPOS

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Manage → Payment Types">
    Click **Payment Types** in the left sidebar under Manage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create or edit a payment type">
    Create a new payment type with code `SC_EPAY` (or your preference) and name `Online Payment`, or edit an existing one. This should be a payment type dedicated to online/QR ordering — keep it separate from any payment type you use for the in-person Share Commerce terminal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Share Commerce ePay processor">
    In the Processors section, click **Add processor**, then select **Share Commerce ePay** from the dropdown (it may be listed as "ShareCommerce E-Payment" — look for the one asking for a Merchant ID, not the one asking for a terminal IP address).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your Merchant ID, Secret Key, and Environment">
    Fill in:
    - **Merchant ID**: From Step 1
    - **Secret Key**: From Step 1
    - **Environment**: `Staging` while testing, `Production` once you go live — this must match the credentials you pasted above
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Processors">
    Click **Save Processors**. Your secret key is encrypted with AES-256 before saving and never stored in plain text.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  For **production** use, make sure your Merchant ID and Secret Key were issued for **live** transactions, and that Environment is set to `Production` — pairing live credentials with `Staging` (or vice versa) will make every payment attempt fail.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Configure Redirect URL in Share Commerce (Optional but Recommended)

For the fastest customer experience, configure the redirect URL so that Share Commerce sends customers back to your QR ordering page immediately after payment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Share Commerce Settings">
    Log back into the Share Commerce Merchant Portal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Webhooks & Redirects">
    Go to **Settings → Webhooks & Redirects** or **Checkout Configuration**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the Redirect URL">
    Enter the following URL:
    ```
    https://api.yourdomain.com/api/{tenantDomain}/qr/payment/sharecommerce/callback
    ```
    Replace `yourdomain.com` with your actual domain and `{tenantDomain}` with your tenant identifier (e.g., `default_tenant` for testing).

    Example:
    ```
    https://api.example.com/api/default_tenant/qr/payment/sharecommerce/callback
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save your redirect configuration. The redirect URL will now automatically be used after every payment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you don't configure a redirect URL, Share Commerce will not redirect customers back to your ordering page. Instead, the webhook path (see Step 4) becomes the only confirmation method.
</Note>

## Step 4: Configure Webhook (Optional but Recommended for Safety)

For maximum reliability, Share Commerce can also send a server-to-server payment confirmation webhook to your API. This acts as a backup in case the customer's browser closes or the redirect fails.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Share Commerce Webhooks">
    In the Share Commerce Merchant Portal, go to **Settings → API & Webhooks**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Webhook Endpoint">
    Create a new webhook with:
    - **Event**: `payment.completed` or similar
    - **URL**: `https://api.yourdomain.com/api/payment-webhooks/sharecommerce`
    - **Method**: `POST`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Signature Verification">
    Check the box to enable **HMAC Signature Verification** or **Secure Webhook Signature**. This is critical for security.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the Webhook">
    Use the **Send Test Webhook** button in Share Commerce to verify the webhook reaches your server. Check your server logs for confirmation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The webhook URL is **public and domain-less** — it serves all tenants. Do NOT add your tenant domain to the webhook URL. DuitPOS resolves your tenant automatically from the payment intent (falling back to a Merchant ID lookup if the intent has already expired). There is no separate Webhook Secret to enter anywhere — the same Secret Key you set in Step 2 is used to verify the webhook's signature.
</Warning>

## How Share Commerce ePay Payments Work

After setup, the payment flow is fully automatic:

### QR Code Ordering (Guest Checkout)

1. Customer scans QR code → opens ordering page
2. Adds items, proceeds to checkout
3. Selects the **Share Commerce ePay** payment type
4. DuitPOS creates a payment intent and redirects to Share Commerce checkout
5. Customer completes payment at Share Commerce
6. Share Commerce redirects back to DuitPOS → ticket is created automatically
7. Customer sees order confirmation with receipt

### Dual-Path Reliability

DuitPOS uses two independent confirmation methods:

| Path | What Happens | Speed |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **PATH A (Redirect)** | After payment, Share Commerce redirects customer's browser back to your QR page. DuitPOS verifies the payment and creates the ticket immediately. | <500ms — instant |
| **PATH B (Webhook)** | Share Commerce sends a server-to-server webhook with the payment confirmation. DuitPOS processes it in the background. | A few seconds — safety net |

Both paths include **idempotency checks** — if the customer's browser refresh hits PATH A twice, or PATH A + PATH B race, DuitPOS ensures only one ticket is created.

## Verifying Your Setup

### Test Payment (Staging)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use Share Commerce staging credentials">
    In Share Commerce, use your **staging** Merchant ID and Secret Key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the DuitPOS payment type">
    Edit your Share Commerce ePay payment type and confirm the Merchant ID, Secret Key, and Environment (`Staging`) are all set correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test a QR order">
    Scan a test QR code, add items, select Share Commerce ePay, and complete a test payment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check payment status">
    After payment, verify that:
    - The redirect returned you to the DuitPOS order summary page
    - The ticket was created with status **PAID**
    - The payment method shows **Share Commerce ePay** in the receipt
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Production Deployment

Once testing is successful:

1. **Switch to live credentials**: Update your Share Commerce ePay payment type with your **production** Merchant ID and Secret Key, and set Environment to `Production`
2. **Verify redirect URL**: Confirm the redirect URL in Share Commerce is set to your **production domain** (not localhost)
3. **Test a live payment**: Complete a small test transaction with a real card or e-wallet to ensure everything works end-to-end
4. **Monitor logs**: Check your server logs for any webhook errors during the first few days

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Payment shows as expired but I paid successfully">
    This usually means the redirect URL is not configured in Share Commerce. The customer paid, but your server did not receive confirmation.

    **Fix**: Go to Share Commerce Settings → Checkout Configuration and set your Redirect URL (see Step 3 above). The webhook (Step 4) will catch future payments as a backup.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="'Invalid Share Commerce Signature' error">
    Your Secret Key in DuitPOS does not match the Secret Key at Share Commerce, or the Environment doesn't match which key you're using.

    **Fix**:
    1. Log into Share Commerce and copy your **current** Secret Key again (make sure you're in the right environment — staging vs. production)
    2. Paste it into DuitPOS (Manage → Payment Types → your Share Commerce ePay payment type → Processors → Share Commerce ePay → Secret Key)
    3. Confirm Environment matches (Staging key → Staging, Production key → Production)
    4. Click **Save Processors**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Webhook is not arriving">
    Share Commerce may not have your correct webhook URL or the webhook signing verification is failing.

    **Fix**:
    1. Double-check the webhook URL in Share Commerce matches exactly: `https://api.yourdomain.com/api/payment-webhooks/sharecommerce` (no trailing slash, no tenant domain)
    2. Make sure webhook signing is enabled in Share Commerce
    3. Click **Send Test Webhook** in Share Commerce to manually trigger one and check your server logs
    4. If you see `Invalid ShareCommerce signature`, verify your Secret Key is correct (see above)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I see past payments and their status?">
    Go to **Manage → Tickets** and search for tickets with status **PAID** and payment type **Share Commerce ePay**. Click on a ticket to see full details including the payment method, amount, and gateway reference.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I selected 'Share Commerce' instead of 'Share Commerce ePay' — what happens?">
    You'll get a config panel asking for a Terminal IP Address, Terminal Port, and Terminal Model instead of Merchant ID and Secret Key — that's the physical countertop terminal processor, and it won't process online payments. Remove it and add **Share Commerce ePay** instead. See [Two Share Commerce Processors](/features/payment-types#two-share-commerce-processors) for the full comparison.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
