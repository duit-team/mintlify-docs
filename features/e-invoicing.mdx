The **E-Invoicing** module integrates with **LHDN MyInvois** to generate, submit, and track e-invoices as required under Malaysia's e-invoice mandate. Businesses above the revenue threshold are legally required to submit e-invoices for all transactions via the MyInvois platform.

## How This Works: the Intermediary Model

DuitPOS submits e-invoices on your behalf as a registered **intermediary** with LHDN — you do **not** need your own MyInvois API Client ID or Client Secret. The platform holds one shared set of OAuth credentials and a signing certificate for everyone (set up by the platform admin, not you). As a tenant, you only need to:

1. Authorize the platform's intermediary account inside your own MyInvois Portal.
2. Fill in your business TIN and location details below.

<Tip>
  If you were expecting a "Client ID / Client Secret" field in your E-Invoicing settings — there isn't one. That configuration is centralized at the platform level. See [LHDN Intermediary Setup](/features/lhdn-admin) if you're a platform admin setting this up for the first time.
</Tip>

## Prerequisites

Before activating e-invoicing, complete the following:

- Your business **TIN** (Tax Identification Number) from LHDN.
- **Authorize the intermediary** in your own MyInvois Portal account: go to Settings → ERP/Intermediary, add the platform's intermediary TIN, and grant both **Document - Submit** and **Document - View** permissions. Without this step, every submission will fail even though your settings look correct.
- Location details completed — **TIN, BRN, MSIC code, and full address** — set under **Manage → Locations → Edit** for each location.
- At least one tax rate configured under **Manage → Tax**.

<Warning>
  Switch from Sandbox to Production only when your integration has been fully tested. Production submissions are legally binding and cannot be reversed without the formal cancellation process. Always test in Sandbox mode first.
</Warning>

## E-Invoicing Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Authorize the intermediary in MyInvois Portal">
    Log in to your business's own MyInvois Portal account (not DuitPOS) and add the platform's intermediary TIN under Settings → ERP/Intermediary, granting Document - Submit and Document - View permissions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to E-Invoicing → Settings">
    Click **Settings** under the E-Invoicing section in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Environment">
    Choose **Sandbox** for testing or **Production** for live submissions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your Supplier TIN">
    Fill in your company's **TIN** registered with LHDN. This is the only identity field you need — no API credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your location details">
    Go to **Manage → Locations → Edit** for each location and fill in the TIN, BRN, MSIC code, and complete address. These are required for every e-invoice submission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## E-Invoice Documents

The **Documents** page shows all e-invoices submitted or pending submission. For each invoice you can:

- **View details** — see line items, tax breakdown, and submission status.
- **View QR** — display or print the QR code for the e-invoice (required to be shown to customers).
- **View on MyInvois** — open the document directly in the LHDN portal for official verification.

### Invoice Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Generated but not yet submitted to MyInvois |
| **Submitted** | Sent to MyInvois and awaiting validation (usually takes a few seconds) |
| **Valid** | Accepted and validated by LHDN |
| **Invalid** | Rejected by LHDN — view the error details to understand the reason |
| **Cancelled** | Cancelled after submission using the formal cancellation process |

## Submitting an Invoice

E-invoices are generated automatically when a ticket is completed and payment is received. If a submission fails due to a network issue or credential problem, it will be retried automatically. You can also trigger a manual resubmit from the Documents page by clicking **Resubmit** on any Pending or Invalid invoice.

<Tip>
  If invoices are consistently showing as Invalid, check the error message shown on the document detail page. Common reasons include missing or incorrect TIN, invalid MSIC code, or mismatched tax amounts. Fix the issue in Settings or Locations and resubmit.
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When does my business need to start submitting e-invoices?">
    Malaysia's LHDN e-invoice mandate is being rolled out in phases based on annual revenue. Check the LHDN website or consult your accountant for the applicable date for your business size.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if an e-invoice submission fails?">
    Failed submissions are retried automatically up to three times. If all retries fail, the invoice moves to **Invalid** status with an error message. Fix the underlying issue (e.g. incorrect credentials or missing location details) and click **Resubmit**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I cancel a submitted e-invoice?">
    Yes, but only within the permitted cancellation window defined by LHDN (typically 72 hours after the issue date). Go to the document detail page and click **Cancel**. After the window closes, cancellations must be handled through a credit note.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need a MyInvois Client ID and Client Secret?">
    No. DuitPOS submits on your behalf as a registered intermediary, using one shared set of platform-level credentials. You only need your business TIN and to authorize the intermediary in your own MyInvois Portal account — see Prerequisites above.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My submissions fail with an authorization or permission error even though my TIN is correct — why?">
    This almost always means the intermediary authorization step was skipped or one of the two required permissions wasn't granted. Log in to your MyInvois Portal account, go to Settings → ERP/Intermediary, and confirm both **Document - Submit** and **Document - View** are granted to the platform's intermediary TIN.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
