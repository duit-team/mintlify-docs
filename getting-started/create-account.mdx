Creating a DuitPOS account is the first step to getting your restaurant or business up and running. In this guide, you'll set up your account in just a few minutes and receive instant access to your dashboard.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/create-account.png" alt="DuitPOS signup form" />
</Frame>

## What You Need

Before you start, have the following information ready:

- **Your full name** — your personal name as the account owner
- **Email address** — used for login and password resets; must be unique across DuitPOS
- **Business name** — the name of your restaurant, cafe, or business (appears on receipts and reports)
- **Business type** — category describing your business (Restaurant, Cafe, Bakery, Retail, Takeaway, Catering, or Other)
- **Location/Country** — where your business operates (affects currency, tax rules, and payment options available)
- **Phone number** — for support contact and billing inquiries
- **A strong password** — at least 8 characters with uppercase, lowercase, and numbers

## Creating Your Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the signup page">
    Open your browser and navigate to the DuitPOS signup page (typically `https://app.duitpos.com/signup`). If you already have an account, go to the sign-in page instead.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your full name">
    Type your first and last name in the **Full Name** field. This is how you will appear in the system as the account owner.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your email address">
    Type the email address you'll use to log in. This must be unique — you cannot use the same email across multiple DuitPOS accounts. Make sure the spelling is correct, as password reset links are sent here.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your business name">
    Type the name of your restaurant or business in the **Business Name** field. This appears on sales receipts, reports, and anywhere your business is referenced in the system.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your business type">
    Click the **Business Type** dropdown and choose the category that best describes your business. Options include:
    - **Restaurant** — full-service dining
    - **Cafe** — beverage and light meal focus
    - **Bakery** — baked goods and pastries
    - **Retail** — retail merchandise
    - **Takeaway** — counter service only
    - **Catering** — off-site events and functions
    - **Other** — if none of the above fit

    Your selection configures default settings like tax rates and order modes. You can change this later.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your country">
    Click the **Country** dropdown and select the country where your business is located. Available options include Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines, United States, United Kingdom, and Australia. Your country selection determines:
    - The currency (e.g. USD for United States, MYR for Malaysia)
    - Applicable tax rules (GST, VAT, SST, etc.)
    - Which payment processors are available
    - Time zone and language defaults
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your phone number">
    Click the **Phone Number** field. The country's dial code (e.g. +60 for Malaysia, +1 for USA) is automatically filled in based on your country selection. Type the remaining digits of your phone number. For example, if you select Malaysia and your number is 0123456789, you would see `+60123456789`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your password">
    Type a strong password in the **Password** field. The password must meet these requirements:
    - At least 8 characters long
    - Contains at least one uppercase letter (A–Z)
    - Contains at least one number (0–9)

    A **password strength meter** appears below the field showing 1 to 4 bars as you type. Green (4 bars) is strongest. If you want to include special characters (! @ # $ etc.), they're optional but recommended.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your password">
    Type the same password again in the **Confirm Password** field to avoid typos.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Create Account">
    Review all the information you've entered, then click the **Create Account** button. The system will validate your input and create your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Happens Next

After you click **Create Account**, a confirmation screen appears showing "Check your email." Here's what happens:

1. **Verification email sent** — DuitPOS sends a confirmation email to the address you provided. It contains a verification link that activates your account.

2. **Click the verification link** — Check your email (including spam/junk folder) and click the link in the message. This link is unique to your account and expires after **1 hour** for security.

3. **Account activated** — After you click the link, you are automatically logged in and redirected to your DuitPOS dashboard.

4. **Ready to configure** — Your account is now active and ready for setup. You will land on the dashboard with access to all configuration tools. Follow the [Setup Checklist](/getting-started/setup-checklist) to configure your locations, payment methods, tax rates, and menu.

<Tip>
  The verification link expires 1 hour after signup. If you miss it or the link doesn't work, you can request a new one by clicking **Resend verification email** on the confirmation screen.
</Tip>

## Important Notes

<Tip>
  **Email is permanent.** You cannot change your email address after signup. Choose carefully and make sure the spelling is correct — this is your login username.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Choose a strong password.** Your password protects your entire business account and all of your sales data, customer information, and financial records. Never share it with anyone, not even DuitPOS support staff.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  **The verification link expires in 1 hour.** If you close your browser or don't click the link within this time window, you will need to request a new link. This is a security measure to prevent unauthorized account activation.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  **You cannot use the same email twice.** Each DuitPOS account must have a unique email address. If you receive an error saying your email is already registered, try signing in with that email instead. If you've truly forgotten which account uses that email, contact support.
</Warning>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change my email after signup?">
    No. Email is permanent and cannot be changed after your account is created. It serves as your unique login identifier. If you used the wrong email by mistake, you will need to create a new account with the correct email address.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I change my business name after signup?">
    Yes. You can change your business name, business type, and phone number at any time under **Account Settings** (typically found under your profile menu). Your country and email remain permanent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I don't receive the verification email?">
    Check your spam, junk, or promotions folder — it may have been filtered. If you still don't see it, click **Resend verification email** on the confirmation screen. If emails never arrive, check that you entered your email address correctly during signup. If the problem persists, contact DuitPOS support.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I have multiple DuitPOS accounts?">
    Yes, but each account must use a different email address. This is useful if you manage multiple businesses or restaurants. Each account has its own login credentials, data, and configuration.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I forgot my verification link?">
    Click **Resend verification email** on the "Check your email" confirmation screen. A new link will be sent to your email address. The new link also expires after 1 hour.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the password strength meter mean?">
    The strength meter has 4 bars:
    - **1 bar (red)** — weak; doesn't meet requirements
    - **2 bars (orange)** — fair; meets minimum requirements
    - **3 bars (green)** — good; includes uppercase, lowercase, and numbers
    - **4 bars (green)** — strong; includes special characters or very long length

    Aim for 3–4 bars for the best security.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
